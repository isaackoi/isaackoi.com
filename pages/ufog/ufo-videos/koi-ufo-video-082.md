### 3. The real background to this video

The footage in this video is extracted from a music video entitled "Rubber Johnny". The video was directed by [British video artist Chris Cunningham](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chris_Cunningham). It features music by [Aphex Twin (the stage name for Richard David James](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aphex_Twin), a British electronic musician and composer - specifically Aphex's track "Afx 237 v.7" from the album "Drukqs"[)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aphex_Twin). The full "Rubber Johnny" video lasts 6 minutes and shows the relevant figure talking and dancing to Aphex Twin's The humanoid figure in the video is in fact the director, Chris Cunningham, wearing prosthetic makeup created by Neill Gorton.

The full "Rubber Johnny" video is embedded below.

[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FW-ZPTW4_IU](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FW-ZPTW4_IU)

"Rubber Johnny" [has a page on the IMDB movie website](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0441787/) which lists this video as an animated horror short, with a relatively high rating from IMDB users (with a score of 7.4 out of 10). The IMDB website describes "Rubber Johnny" as "An experimental music video about a wheelchair-bound boy and morphs and moves to an Aphex Twin song. The experience gets weirder when he takes cocaine and everything gets out of control".

Snippets of the video are displayed on [Chris Cunningham's own website](http://www.chriscunningham.com/).

![](/images/stories/ufo_videos/video082_10.JPG)

Chris Cunningham discussed his "Rubber Johnny" video in [an interview for the Pitchfork website](http://pitchfork.com/features/interviews/6103-chris-cunningham/), including commenting that it was "just horrible" wearing the head prosthetic in the video and that "It took about an hour to glue the rubber headpiece on and getting in the wheelchair was difficult."

Neill Gorton has worked on various movies (including "Saving Private Ryan" (1998) and "Children of Men" (2006)).
