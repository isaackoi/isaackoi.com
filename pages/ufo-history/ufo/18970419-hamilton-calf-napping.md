Alleged airship encounter on 19 April 1897 involving attempted calf-napping on the ranch of Alexander Hamilton atLe Roy,Kansas (sometimes referred to asLeRoy,Kansas).

**Claims to fame**

This incident is **Case 24** in [Isaac Koi's "Top 100" article](/ufog/best-ufo-cases/13-the-top-100-ufo-cases), since it was referred to in **74** of the books covered by that article.

Isaac Koi’s [“ICES” Rating](/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings) for this sighting = **3,360** (out of a potential score of 14*14*14*14 i.e. 38,416)

(1) [“Impact” Rating](/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings#impact) of **14** (out of a potential score of 14), because it is Case 24 in [Isaac Koi's "Top 100" article](/ufog/best-ufo-cases/13-the-top-100-ufo-cases).

(2) [“Credibility” Rating](/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings#credibility) of **4** (out of a potential score of 14) because several researchers have identified convincing evidence this sighting was a hoax.

(2) [“Expert” Rating](/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings#expert) of **5** (out of a potential score of 14). It is given this relatively low rating because (at the time of writing) it has not been included in any of the short lists of the best cases produced by various UFO researchers that have been compiled by Isaac Koi (see the “[Best UFO Cases](/ufog/best-ufo-cases)" article at [PART 3: Existing lists by various individuals](/ufog/best-ufo-cases/3-experts-short-lists)), and is it is mentioned relatively often as an example of a hoax.

(4)[“Strangeness” Rating](/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings#strangeness) of **12** (out of a potential score of 14). The sighting has a relatively high strangeness rating since it would be very difficult to explain this sighting as a misperception or misidentification..
