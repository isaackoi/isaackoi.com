On 25th February 1942,Los Angeles was blacked out whilst U.S. Army gunners fire at a UFO overLos Angeles andBurbank,California,USA (commonly referred to as the “Battle ofLos Angeles").

This incident is Case 95 in [Isaac Koi's "Top 100" article]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/13-the-top-100-ufo-cases' | relative_url }}), since it was referred to in 29 of the books covered by that article

**Claims to fame**

This incident is **Case 95** in [Isaac Koi's "Top 100" article]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/13-the-top-100-ufo-cases' | relative_url }}), since it was referred to in **29** of the books covered by that article.

Isaac Koi’s [“ICES” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}) for this sighting = **8,232** (out of a potential score of 14*14*14*14 i.e. 38,416)

(1) [“Impact” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#impact) of **14** (out of a potential score of 14), because it is Case 95 in [Isaac Koi's "Top 100" article]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/13-the-top-100-ufo-cases' | relative_url }}).

(2) [“Credibility” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#credibility) of **14** (out of a potential score of 14) because shooting clearly did take place on the relevant night. The debate has centered on what (if anything at all) was actually there to be shot at.

(2) [“Expert” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#expert) of **7** (out of a potential score of 14). It is given this neutral rating because (at the time of writing) it has not been included in any of the short lists of the best cases produced by various UFO researchers that have been compiled by Isaac Koi (see the “[Best UFO Cases]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases' | relative_url }})" article at [PART 3: Existing lists by various individuals]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/3-experts-short-lists' | relative_url }})), but also is not commonly given by UFO researchers as an example of an incident that can be explained by hoax, misperception or other mundane cause.

(4)[“Strangeness” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#strangeness) of **6** (out of a potential score of 14). The sighting has a relatively low strangeness rating since there is relatively limited evidence to be explained as to the nature of the object (if any at all) in the sky that night.
