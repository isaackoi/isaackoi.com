On 7 January [1948]({{ '/tags/1948' | relative_url }}), Captain Thomas F Mantell Jr (Air National Guard F-51 Mustang fighter pilot) dies during attempted intercept near Godman Air Force Base (near Fort Knox), Kentucky.

**Claims to fame**

This incident is **Case 6** in [Isaac Koi's "Top 100" article]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/13-the-top-100-ufo-cases' | relative_url }}), since it was referred to in **179** of the books covered by that article.

Isaac Koi’s [“ICES” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}) for this sighting = **5,824** (out of a potential score of 14*14*14*14 i.e. 38,416)

(1) [“Impact” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#impact) of **14** (out of a potential score of 14), because it is **Case 6** in [Isaac Koi's "Top 100" article]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/13-the-top-100-ufo-cases' | relative_url }}).

(2) [“Credibility” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#credibility) of **13** (out of a potential score of 14) because an object was reported by several people and Mantell died while attempting to chase it. The debate has centered on what he was actually attempting to chase.

(2) [“Expert” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#expert) of **8** (out of a potential score of 14). It is given this slightly above neutral rating because while (at the time of writing) it has not been included in any of the short lists of the best cases produced by various UFO researchers that have been compiled by Isaac Koi (see the “[Best UFO Cases]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases' | relative_url }})" article at [PART 3: Existing lists by various individuals]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/3-experts-short-lists' | relative_url }})) this incident did come fifth in [a survey in 1965 by Jacques Vallee]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/5-consensus-lists-jacques-vallees-poll-1965' | relative_url }}) of the opinion of various UFO groups as to the most significant UFO sightings..

(4)[“Strangeness” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#strangeness) of **4** (out of a potential score of 14). The sighting has a relatively low strangeness rating since there is relatively limited evidence to be explained as to the nature of the object in the sky. That evidence is largely consistent with a large balloon.
