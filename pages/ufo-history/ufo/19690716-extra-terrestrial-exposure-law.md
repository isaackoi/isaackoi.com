*Extra-Terrestrial Exposure Law*

On 16 July

1969

, the United States adopted Title 14, Section 1211 of the Code of Federal Regulations (“14 CFR 1211”), since known as the “Extra-Terrestrial Exposure Law”, applicable to “

all

NASA

manned and unmanned space missions which land or come within the atmospheric envelope of a celestial body and return to the Earth”.

Revoked in April

1991

. (Commonly referred to as “the ET law” or “the ET exposure law”.)
