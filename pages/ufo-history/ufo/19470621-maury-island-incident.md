On 21 June

1947

, near Maury Island, Washington there was an alleged sighting and claims of debris from a UFO involving Harold A Dahl with a related alleged sighting the following day by Dahl’s superior – Fred Lee Crisman.

Dahl and Crisman are often referred to as harbor patrolmen.

The incident is commonly referred to as “the Maury Island hoax” or “the Maury Island incident”.

**Claims to fame**

This incident is **Case 12** in [Isaac Koi's "Top 100" article]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/13-the-top-100-ufo-cases' | relative_url }}), since it was referred to in **99** of the books covered by that article.

Isaac Koi’s [“ICES” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}) for this sighting = **4,368** (out of a potential score of 14*14*14*14 i.e. 38,416)

(1) [“Impact” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#impact) of **14** (out of a potential score of 14), because it is Case 95 in [Isaac Koi's "Top 100" article]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/13-the-top-100-ufo-cases' | relative_url }}).

(2) [“Credibility” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#credibility) of **3** (out of a potential score of 14) because many researchers have put forward compelling evidence that this incident was merely a hoax.

(2) [“Expert” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#expert) of **8** (out of a potential score of 14). It is given this slightly above neutral rating because (at the time of writing) it has included in one of the short lists of the best cases produced by various UFO researchers that have been compiled by Isaac Koi (see the “[Best UFO Cases]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases' | relative_url }})" article at [PART 3: Existing lists by various individuals]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/3-experts-short-lists' | relative_url }})).

(4)[“Strangeness” Rating]({{ '/ufog/best-ufo-cases/27-quantitative-criteria-kois-ices-ratings' | relative_url }}#strangeness) of **13** (out of a potential score of 14). The sighting has a very strangeness rating since the witness accounts, if not hoaxed, would be very difficult to reconcile with a mundane explanation.
